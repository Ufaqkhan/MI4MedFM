Overview

Why MI4MedFM matters

Medical foundation models are rapidly becoming core infrastructure for medical imaging and multimodal clinical AI. Yet high-stakes use requires more than post-hoc explanations. MI4MedFM focuses on mechanistic interpretability—reverse-engineering the underlying algorithms learned by models. By emphasizing rigorous internal analysis via techniques like sparse autoencoders (SAEs), circuit discovery, and activation patching, we aim to transform black-box medical foundation models into robust, auditable clinical systems.

The workshop is designed to bridge mechanistic interpretability researchers with the MICCAI community, including clinicians, safety researchers, and industry practitioners, and to build a shared agenda for robust, clinically grounded evaluation and deployment.

Submission & review

Two-track submission with rigorous peer review

The workshop uses an OpenReview-based, double-blind review process with clearly differentiated tracks for original work and non-archival contributions.

Track 1

Proceedings track

Original work intended for the MICCAI Satellite Events joint Springer LNCS proceedings.

Track 2

Non-archival track

Extended abstracts, previously published work, or work under review elsewhere.

Review framework

How submissions are evaluated

  1. Double-blind review Each submission receives at least 2 reviews, and 3 when feasible.
  2. Meta-review oversight Senior PC or area chairs synthesize feedback and support decisions.
  3. Poster-first presentation All accepted works are presented as posters; a subset is selected for oral talks.
  4. COI safeguards Conflicted organizers do not handle submissions from the same institution or recent collaborators.

FAQ

Quick answers for prospective attendees and authors

Who should attend MI4MedFM?

Researchers in medical imaging and clinical AI, clinicians, machine learning interpretability and safety researchers, and industry practitioners working with medical foundation models.

What kinds of submissions fit the workshop?

Original proceedings-track papers, as well as non-archival extended abstracts, previously published work, or work currently under review.

How will accepted work be presented?

All accepted works will appear as posters, with a subset selected for oral presentation based on review quality and topic balance.

What makes this workshop different from other MedFM events?

Its focus is on internal mechanisms, clinical validation, and intervention-ready insight rather than only model development, benchmarking, or post-hoc explanations.
